About me

I am a MSCA Ph.D candidate at the Sorbonne Center for Artificial Intelligence (SCAI). I belong to a mixed research unit named STMS, which stands for Science and Technology of Music and Sound. My office is located on the Institut de Recherche et Coordination Acoustique-Musique (IRCAM), next to the Centre Pompidou.

The goal of my thesis is to generate realistic sounds with controllable pitch, timbre and expresiveness. To tackle this problem, classical methods (concatenative synthesis, physical modeling, signal-based methods,...) as well as deep learning end-to-end methods (WaveNet, DDSP,...) are found in literature. The hypothesis of my thesis is that incorporating physics-priors into deep learning methods reduces the space of possible solutions and provides interpretable parameters. In particular, the implemented methodology to learn the physical constraints is the Port-Hamiltonian formulation, developed around energy balance and passivity.

Starting my second year of the thesis, I would love to dedicate my working life to machine learning research and its different applications. My biggest passions are music, cinema and languages.
